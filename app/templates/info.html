What is this?

This program displays data from 5eTools (https://5e.tools/index.html), an open source rules repository for the game Dungeons & Dragons 5th edition.
The game publishes official rules and content across many books. This site aggregates them into one place.
This program scrapes data contained in JSON files in 5eTools' backend /data directory and allows the user to browse through it.
A brief explanation of the game itself can be found here (https://dnd.wizards.com/basics-play).
This program provides an interface for browsing the game's character Races, Backgrounds, Feats, and Spells.

Races:

Every character belongs to a race, one of the many intelligent humanoid species in the D&D world.
The most common player character races are dwarves, elves, halflings, and humans.
The race you choose contributes to your character's identity in an important way, by establishing a general appearance and the natural talents gained from culture and ancestry.
Your character's race grants particular racial traits, such as special senses, proficiency with certain weapons or tools, proficiency in one or more skills, or the ability to use minor spells.
Your race also increases one or more of your ability scores.

Backgrounds:

When creating a character, players usually choose a predefined background archetype that aligns to the backstory that they have in mind for their character.
Ex. A character raised in a church by the clergy might choose the Acolyte background, or a rogue who lived on the streets the Urchin background.
Backgrounds provide a character certain features and determine what languages they speak, skills and tools they are trained in, etc.

Feats:

A feat represents a talent or an area of expertise that gives a character special capabilities.
It embodies training, experience, and special abilities.
They provide the character various unique features and bonuses, and can grant proficiencies and ability score increases.

Spells:

Some characters have the ability to perform magic spells to change the world around them, as simple as creating a puff of smoke or as powerful as changing reality as a whole.
Spellcaster characters choose from the list of spells which ones that they have access to at any point in time.

Form Fields:

Name: Filter for items whose names begin with the entered query.
Source: Filter items by the source material book that they were printed in.
Language Proficiencies:Languages that the character can speak, ex. Elvish, Dwarvish, or Gnomish.
Ability Bonus: Characters' strengths are defined by 6 ability scores:
  • Strength (str),
  • Dexterity (dex),
  • Constitution (con),
  • Intelligence (int),
  • Wisdom (wis),
  • Charisma (cha).
Players roll these stats when creating their character. When a character attempts to do something in the game, they usually have to roll dice to determine if that action succeeds.
When they do that, they add/subtract whatever modifiers there are based on their character's talent in that area and any other circumstances. Ex. a character attempting to haggle with a merchant might roll whats called a Charisma Check to determine if they succeed.
Skill Proficiencies: Skills refer to specialized areas that fall underneath an ability score, ex. skill in acrobatics falling under Dexterity or skill in History falling under intelligence. Proficiency in a skill provides a bonus when rolling to perform actions having to do with that skill, Ex. Athletics proficieny making it easier to climb a rocky cliff.
Tool Proficiencies: Proficiency with a tool provides a bonus when rolling to perform actions using that tool, Ex. Theive's Tools proficieny making it easier to pick a lock.
Size: Races belong to one of three size categories: Small, Medium, Large.
Duration: Some spells resolve their effects instantaneously, some have lingering effects that last a certain amount of time.
Level: Spells have a numeric level 0 through 9 that signifies their power. As a spellcaster character gains levels, they gain access to stronger, higher level spells.
School: A broad category to which spells belong based on their effects.
  • Conjuration - Creating things out of thin air.
  • Necromancy - Manipulating life and death.
  • Evocation - Destructive magic
  • Abjuration - Protective magic
  • Transmutation - Transforming things into other things.
  • Divination - Revealing knowledge
  • Enchantment - Mental manipulation
  • Illusion - Creating illusions
